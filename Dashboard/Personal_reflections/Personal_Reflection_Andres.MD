# Personal Reflection: GreenGrid Dashboard — Phase 2
**Student:** Andrés González Gómez 
**Team:** Team 3  
**Date:** April 02, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Phase 2 allowed me to focus on the part of the project I enjoy the most: turning technical work into a clear and visually engaging experience. While Phase 1 was more technical, this phase was about presentation and communication. My main contribution was shaping the dashboard so it felt polished, intuitive, and easy to understand.

---

## My Role in Phase 2

My role in this phase centered on the dashboard’s frontend design, visual structure, and presentation flow. I was mainly responsible for building and refining the interface, organizing how each section should appear, and making sure the final result felt cohesive rather than just functional. That included the overall layout, section hierarchy, chart placement, visual transitions, spacing, styling decisions, and many of the details that made the dashboard feel like a finished product instead of a prototype.

---

## What Marked Me Most About This Phase

What stood out to me the most was how much effort goes into making something feel “simple.” At first glance, a dashboard can look like just a collection of charts and sections, but once I started shaping the interface more carefully, I realized how many tiny decisions influence whether the user feels oriented or overwhelmed. Something as basic as spacing, color contrast, card size, or how one section transitions into the next can completely change the experience.

That was one of the most interesting parts of this phase for me. I enjoyed thinking about the dashboard not only as a place to display data, but as something with pacing and visual intention. I found myself paying much more attention to the emotional side of presentation: what feels heavy, what feels clean, what creates clarity, and what helps the story land better.

---

## What I Learned About Myself

This phase reminded me that I naturally think a lot about presentation, aesthetics, and user experience, even in technical projects. I tend to care deeply about whether something not only works, but also feels polished and communicates clearly. That is something I have noticed in other creative and technical work I do, but this project made it especially obvious.

I also realized that I enjoy the process of refining and iterating much more than I expected. A lot of this phase was not about building something from scratch in one shot, but about gradually improving it until it felt right. That kind of work can sometimes seem minor from the outside, but I learned that it is actually one of the most important parts of making a project feel professional.

---

## Looking Forward

Moving into future phases and projects, I want to continue strengthening that intersection between technical implementation and visual communication. I think one of the biggest lessons I am taking from GreenGrid is that even the strongest data or simulation can lose impact if it is not presented in a way that people can immediately understand and connect with.

---

## Conclusion

Phase 2 helped transform the project from something functional into something polished and cohesive. My main contribution was improving how the dashboard looks and feels as a whole, making it more clear and effective for the user.
